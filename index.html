Research Experience

2026

Stage-wise NWP Integration for Wind Power Forecasting

Principal Investigator · Undergraduate Innovation Training Program

Investigated the role of Numerical Weather Prediction (NWP) information at different stages of wind power forecasting, addressing the lack of a unified understanding of NWP integration strategies. Proposed a stage-wise framework that incorporates NWP through representation learning, latent-level fusion, and forecast correction, together with an NWP bias-correction strategy to improve forecast quality. Conducted extensive experiments across multiple forecasting horizons, NWP error levels, and wind fluctuation scenarios to evaluate the strengths and limitations of different integration stages. Preliminary results demonstrate that the choice of NWP integration stage significantly affects forecasting accuracy and model robustness, providing practical guidance for the design of NWP-aware wind power forecasting models.

Time Series Representation Learning Feature Fusion NWP
2025

NIPT-Based Fetal Anomaly Detection & Optimal Screening Timing

Programmer · CUMCM Mathematical Modeling Contest

To address a noninvasive prenatal testing optimization problem in the 2025 CUMCM, 1) multiple regression was used to model and test the relationship between DNA concentration, gestational age, and BMI; 2) K-means clustering was employed to optimize the timing through BMI grouping; 3) random survival forest was utilized to integrate age and height for predicting success rates, optimizing the detection window and handling censored data; 4) random forest classification was applied to achieve high-dimensional discrimination of trisomy in female fetuses.
